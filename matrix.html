Step 3: Get Your Recommendation

üü¢ SCENARIO A: Continue Independently (No Tutor Needed Yet)

Why: Your child has demonstrated both ability and commitment. They can handle 11+ material and maintain a routine.


Recommended Path:

  • Continue with similar story-based challenges
  • Introduce official 11+ practice papers (1-2 per week)
  • Build vocabulary through reading
  • Consider a tutor in 3-6 months ONLY if scores plateau

Save Money: You can likely prepare independently until closer to the exam (Spring 2026), saving ¬£1,000-¬£3,000 in unnecessary early tutoring.

üü° SCENARIO B: Selective Tutoring (Strategic Support)

Why: Your child has potential but needs help with specific areas or technique refinement.


Recommended Path:

  • Continue independent practice for areas of strength
  • Hire a tutor for 1 hour per week focusing ONLY on weak areas you identified
  • Give the tutor a specific brief (e.g., "focus on verbal reasoning patterns and vocabulary")
  • Re-evaluate every 6 weeks

Smart Spending: Targeted tutoring (¬£40-60/week) is far more effective than general tutoring (¬£80-120/week) when you know exactly what needs work.

üü° SCENARIO C: Address Motivation First (Hold Off on Tutoring)

Why: Your child CAN do the work but WON'T. A tutor won't fix motivation issues and may make things worse by creating pressure and resentment.


Recommended Path:

  • Have an honest conversation about whether THEY want to try for the 11+
  • If yes, work on building intrinsic motivation before hiring a tutor
  • Try reward systems for completing daily practice (not for scores)
  • Make learning more game-like and less "school-like"
  • Only hire a tutor once they're doing work willingly

Warning: Hiring a tutor when motivation is low often leads to: wasted money (¬£2,000+), family conflict, and damaged confidence. Fix motivation first.

üî¥ SCENARIO D: Build Foundations First (Not Ready for Tutoring Yet)

Why: Your child is struggling with basic comprehension and reasoning. They need foundational literacy skills before tackling 11+ content.


Recommended Path:

  • Focus on daily reading (20 minutes) at their current level
  • Play word games (Scrabble, Boggle, crosswords)
  • Watch educational videos together and discuss
  • Consider a literacy tutor (NOT an 11+ tutor) to build core reading skills
  • Re-assess in 3 months with another challenge or assessment

Honest Truth: Right now, an 11+ tutor would be working above your child's level and could damage their confidence. Build basics first, then decide if 11+ is the right path.

Your Action Plan

Based on your scenario, here's what to do in the next 7 days:


  • Day 1-2: Review your observations from the challenge and identify which scenario fits your child
  • Day 3: Have a conversation with your child about what they found easy/hard and whether they enjoyed it
  • Day 4-5: Research your recommended path (find more challenges, research tutors in your area, look for reading programs, etc.)
  • Day 6-7: Make your decision and take the first step on your chosen path

üí° Key Insight

The RIGHT time to hire a tutor is when your child has foundational ability AND willingness to work, but needs expert guidance to go from "good" to "excellent." Tutoring too early wastes money. Tutoring too late creates panic. This matrix helps you find the sweet spot.
