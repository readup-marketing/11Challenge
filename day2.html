Chapter Two: The Circle of Masks

The candlelight made strange moving shadows across the marble floor. Six masked figures stood very still in a circle. Each mask was different: a wolf with silver teeth, a fox with golden eyes, an owl with blue jewels, a black raven, a snake with green scales, and a stag with gold antlers.

"Welcome to the Circle of Thornwood," a voice said. Elara couldn't see who was speaking. "You have all been called here because you each have something we need. A gift. A secret. A debtSomething owed to someone else to be paid."

Gran's hand held Elara's shoulder tighter. "I know why you've called me here, but my granddaughterThe daughter of your son or daughter has nothing to do with this. She's just a child."

"No," said the figure in the wolf mask, stepping forward. His voice was deep and strong. "The young Miss Blackwood is exactly why we have gatheredCome together in one place tonight. We knew she would come‚Äîit had to happen."

The figure in the owl mask moved closer. She walked smoothly and quietly. "Tell us, child, have you noticed anything... unusual about yourself? Dreams that feel too real? Can you find lost things? Perhaps you hear whispers when no one else does?"

Elara's breath stopped. How could they know about the whispers? She had never told anyone, not even Gran. The soft voices that seemed to come from old books and ancientVery old objects, telling her secrets and stories.
